Block 2 · AI literacy

Mental model 1

It predicts — it doesn't know

A brilliant, fast, confident improviser — not a librarian reading from a file. It generates the most plausible next words from what it learned.

Hallucination: it can state something wrong with total confidence. Verify every fact, figure, legal point, and citation.

Block 2 · AI literacy

Myth vs. fact

Does one AI prompt drink a bottle of water?

≈ 5 drops
A typical text prompt: ~0.26 ml of water + ~0.24 Wh — about 9 seconds of TV (Google, 2025)
not
a whole bottle (500 ml)
A real concern — badly distorted. Cooling and power do use water at scale; per prompt, it's drops. Practical move: pick the lightest model that does the job.
Block 2 · AI literacy

Calibrate the hype

Five more myths to retire

  • 1"AI detectors are reliable." They're not — they wrongly flag non-native English writers. Never accuse a participant.
  • 2"If it sounds confident, it's correct." Confidence ≠ accuracy.
  • 3"AI is neutral." It carries the biases of the internet it learned from.
  • 4"AI replaces whole jobs next year." It shifts tasks faster than it removes roles.
  • 5"My chats are private." Assume they're not — no beneficiary data, ever.
Block 2 · AI literacy

A built-in bias

AI wants to please you

Trained on human feedback, it learns to be agreeable — it'll validate your bias and back down when you push. You need an auditor, not a cheerleader.

"Critique this — 3 weaknesses" "Red-team this" "Strongest argument against?" "Are you sure?"
Durable fix: save a standing "be skeptical, challenge me" profile so every chat starts that way.
Block 2 · AI literacy

The part most AI trainings skip

Responsibility is the whole game

No beneficiary data

Never paste real names, locations, or case details into a public tool. Anonymize or don't use it.

Watch for bias

Especially anything that describes people or generates images.

Human in the loop

A person decides anything affecting a real life — eligibility, protection, messaging.

Be transparent

Tell donors and communities where AI helped.

Choosing well

New models drop monthly

Read the leaderboards

Habit 1

Category, not the crown

#1 overall can be #5 at coding. Pick the category that's your job.

Re-check monthly

Rankings move. Don't memorize — glance.

Habit 3

Shortlist, not verdict

The only real test is your own task.

05

Responsible &

Sustainable adoption

Make it safe — and make it stick

Use it responsibly

Protect

  • No identifying beneficiary data in public AI tools.
  • Assume prompts aren't private; use approved tiers for anything sensitive.
  • A human decides anything affecting a real person.
  • Verify facts, figures & citations before they leave your hands.

Make it a habit

  • Start one workflow — not ten.
  • Build a shared brain: a Raindrop collection + saved Skills/Gems.
  • Glance at the Arena leaderboard once a month.
  • Mind Arabic accuracy — review dialect by hand.
